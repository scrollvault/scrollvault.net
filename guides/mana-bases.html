Master the fundamentals of building mana bases for Magic: The Gathering. Learn land counts, color balance, and how to use our calculator for any format.

Building a solid mana base is the foundation of any successful Magic deck. Whether you're in Standard, Modern, Pioneer, or Commander, understanding how many lands you need, how to balance your colors, and when to play special lands is critical.

Key Principles

Color Balance: Ensure you have enough sources for each color in your mana cost. Use our Mana Base Calculator to get precise numbers.

Mana Curve: Higher curves need more lands; low curves can shave a few.

Format Considerations: Some formats have fetch lands, shock lands, or fast lands that affect your decisions.

Land Count Guidelines

  • Standard: 24-26 lands (depending on curve)
  • Modern: 24-26 lands with mana fixers
  • Pioneer: 24-26 lands
  • Commander: 36-38 lands on average, with 10+ ramp pieces

Dual Lands and Mana Fixing

Dual lands are the backbone of multicolored decks. Check our Dual Land Cycles guide for a complete list of fetch, shock, check, and fast lands and which formats they're legal in.

Using the Calculator

Our Mana Base Calculator lets you input your deck's color distribution and get tailored land recommendations. It's a great starting point, but always playtest and adjust.
