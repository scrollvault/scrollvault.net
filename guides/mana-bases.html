MTG Manabase Guide — Frank Karsten Mana Math Explained

Your manabase is the foundation of every Magic: The Gathering deck. A well-built manabase ensures you can cast your spells on curve, while a bad one loses games before they start. This guide covers everything you need to know about building the optimal manabase for any format.

Frank Karsten's Mana Math

The gold standard for manabase construction comes from Frank Karsten, a Hall of Fame player and mathematician who published groundbreaking research on MTG mana math. Using hypergeometric probability, Karsten calculated exactly how many colored mana sources you need to consistently cast your spells on curve. His key finding: you need roughly 14 sources to reliably cast a single-pip spell on turn one, and 18+ sources for double-pip costs.

Land Count Guidelines by Format

  • Standard (60 cards): 24–26 lands. Aggro decks can go as low as 22 with a very low curve.
  • Modern (60 cards): 20–24 lands. Fetch lands effectively thin your deck; many Modern decks run fewer lands than Standard.
  • Pioneer (60 cards): 23–26 lands. Similar to Standard but without fetch land thinning.
  • Commander (99+1 cards): 35–38 lands plus 10+ mana rocks and ramp spells. More colors means more dual lands for fixing.
  • Limited (40 cards): 17 lands is the default. Aggressive decks can go to 16; slow decks may want 18.

Colored Source Requirements

Based on Karsten's mana math, here are the colored sources you need in a 60-card deck to cast spells on curve with ~90% reliability:

  • Single pip (1W): 14 sources of that color
  • Double pip (WW): 18 sources
  • Triple pip (WWW): 21+ sources
  • Early single pip (W on turn 1): 14 untapped sources

For Commander, multiply these numbers by roughly 1.6x due to the larger deck size (99 cards). Our manabase calculator handles this math automatically.

Types of Mana Fixing

Dual lands are the backbone of multicolor mana bases. The best dual land cycles (fetch lands, shock lands, original duals) enter untapped and fix multiple colors. See our complete dual land guide for every cycle and their format legality.

Mana rocks like Sol Ring, Arcane Signet, and Signets provide additional fixing, especially in Commander. Count 2-mana rocks as roughly half a land when building your manabase.

Common Manabase Mistakes

  • Running too few colored sources for double-pip costs (e.g., only 12 white sources for a WW card)
  • Playing too many tap lands, which slows your curve by a full turn
  • Ignoring mana curve when choosing land count — a 2.5 average CMC deck needs fewer lands than a 3.5 CMC deck
  • Not accounting for utility lands that don't produce colored mana
