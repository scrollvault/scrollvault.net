MTG Standard Rotation Guide — What Sets Are Legal

Standard rotation is one of the most important events on Magic's calendar. When sets rotate out, entire decks can become illegal overnight, while new archetypes emerge. This guide covers everything you need to know about how Standard rotation works, what's currently legal, and how to plan ahead.

How Standard Rotation Works

Standard uses a rolling window of recent sets. Once per year (typically with the fall set), the oldest sets leave the format. Wizards expanded Standard's window in 2024, meaning more sets are legal at any given time than under the previous 2-year model.

  • Rotation frequency: Once per year (fall set release)
  • Window size: Approximately 3 years of sets (expanded from 2 years in 2024)
  • What rotates: The oldest block of sets leaves when the new fall set enters

Currently Legal Sets

Standard currently includes all Standard-legal sets released from Wilds of Eldraine (September 2023) through the most recent release. This includes:

  • Wilds of Eldraine (September 2023)
  • The Lost Caverns of Ixalan (November 2023)
  • Murders at Karlov Manor (February 2024)
  • Outlaws of Thunder Junction (April 2024)
  • Bloomburrow (August 2024)
  • Duskmourn: House of Horror (September 2024)
  • Foundations (November 2024)
  • Aetherdrift (February 2025)
  • Tarkir: Dragonstorm (April 2025)
  • Final Fantasy (June 2025)

Note: This list is updated periodically. Check Wizards of the Coast for the most current legal sets.

Upcoming 2026 Releases

Several new sets will enter Standard throughout 2026:

  • TMNT (Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles) — Q1 2026
  • Marvel Super Heroes — Q2 2026
  • The Hobbit — Q3 2026
  • Reality Fracture — Q4 2026 (likely rotation set)

Planning for Rotation

  • 3-4 months before rotation: Avoid investing heavily in cards from the oldest legal sets
  • 1 month before: Start testing decks that use only cards from the sets that will survive rotation
  • Rotation day: The new Standard metagame is wide open — early brewers often find the best decks first
  • After rotation: Rotating cards often drop in price but may see play in Pioneer or Modern

Standard on MTG Arena

MTG Arena implements Standard rotation automatically — cards from rotated sets become illegal in Standard queues but remain playable in Historic, Explorer, and other Arena formats. Your collection is never truly lost. For Arena-specific tips, see our MTG Arena Beginner's Guide.
