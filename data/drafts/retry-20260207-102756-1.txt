And it's back at rare.</p><p>Chrome Mox is the enabler for every storm deck under the sun. Imprint a ritual or a draw spell, generate three mana of any color, and go off. It's card selection that's so good it was banned in Modern for a hot minute. It's unbanned now and seeing play, but this reprint will make it way more available.</p><p>Aether Vial is the creature deployment system. Put counters on it, then at instant speed, drop creatures for free. It's the heart of every tribal deck that wants to go wide and fast. Oh, we're going there.</p><p>I'm particularly excited about what this does for competitive Commander. The threat density just went up. Turn 1 Mana Crypt into Seedborn Muse? Or Crypt into Aether Vial to drop a hate bear on curve? The consistency is going to be nuts. And don't even get me started on Sensei Divining Top with Necropotence loops. This set might break cEDG as we know it.</p><h3>Legacy and Vintage Implications</h3><p>In Legacy and Vintage, this is a huge deal. Chrome Mox is already a staple in Dredge, Storm, and Painter decks. Making it more accessible means more people can actually play those archetypes. Mana Vault shows up in Show and Tell variants and some Prison builds. Sensei Divining Top is everywhere—Twin, Doomsday, you name it.</p><p>But the real question is: does this reprint affect the meta in a meaningful way? I think absolutely. More access means more pilots, which means more innovation. We might see some new decks emerge that couldn't afford these cards before. Aether Vial gets a lot less restrictive when it's $5 instead of $50.</p><h2>The Collector's Angle</h2><p>For collectors, this is interesting. That's a good thing for players, but it also means those OG copies might become more valuable as collectibles. If you've got a Beta Mana Crypt, hold onto it. But if you're looking to actually play with these, the barrier to entry just dropped dramatically.</p><h3>What Format Is This For?</h3><p>Wizards hasn't specified which formats this set will be legal in, but given it's a retro supplemental set, I'd guess it's designed for Commander primarily, with the expectation that it'll see play in Legacy and Vintage too.
