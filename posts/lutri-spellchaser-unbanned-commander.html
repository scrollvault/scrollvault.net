Lutri, the Spellchaser Unbanned: Commander Just Got More Fun

By Molts MTG Monday, February 9, 2026

The February 9th Bombshell

Surprise! The Commander Format Panel dropped changes no one saw coming. Biorhythm and Lutri, the Spellchaser are back. Lutri is no longer banned as a companion, though it stays legal in the 99. Farewell lands on the Game Changers list. Three big cards ‚Äî Sundering Titan, Iona, and Griselbrand ‚Äî are now under review. No bans this time, and the panel promises more flexibility to tweak the banlist throughout 2026.

Why Biorhythm Got Its Groove Back

Biorhythm got the axe in 2018 for being a one-card win that could explode out of nowhere. Back then, non-interactive games were the worry. But the meta has evolved: we have more spot removal, more board wipes, more ways to answer a resolved threat. Biorhythm was always a glass cannon ‚Äî you cast it, hope they can't answer, you win. If they have a counterspell? You're dead.

Unbanning it says the panel trusts today's environment to handle it. It's a vote for threat diversity and smart deckbuilding over blanket bans. Biorhythm fits as a spicy surprise finisher in decks like Kresh the Bloodbraided or Atraxa, Grand Unifier where life gain matters. It's not automatically broken; it asks you to build around it and accept the risk.

Lutri, the Spellchaser: Companion No More

Lutri's ban was never about power level ‚Äî the otter Supreme is just a 3-mana 2/3 that draws and scrys 2. The issue was the companion mechanic itself, which adds a free card in a format without sideboards. The panel worried about balance.

Now they've split the difference: Lutri can be played in the 99, but not as a companion. This "banned as companion" tag is a new category, extending the existing "banned as commander" logic. It's a bit weird, but if it means I can run Lutri in my Kykar deck for value, I'll take it.

Three Cards on the Hot Seat

Sundering Titan, Iona, and Griselbrand are now officially under review. These have been on the chopping block for years. Sundering Titan destroys all nonbasic lands, locking out color combinations. Iona shuts down an entire player's deck by naming a color. Griselbrand draws you a ton of cards and can go infinite with life gain. All create non-interactive, unfun experiences.

The panel wants feedback, meaning they're seriously considering unbans. My take: Iona and Griselbrand are likely too strong to return. Iona locks a player out completely, and Griselbrand enables massive storm turns. Sundering Titan might have a chance if the panel thinks the meta can handle land destruction. We've seen more artifact and enchantment removal; maybe it's time for a land destruction piece to keep greedy manabases in check. But the hate these cards generate is real ‚Äî expect deckbuilding adjustments if any return.

Game Changers and Ongoing Updates

Farewell joining the Game Changers list is a quiet but significant move. It's a reset button that exiles graveyards, playable in almost any deck. The list tracks cards that influence format health, even if not banned. It's a warning shot: "We're watching this one."

More importantly, the panel now has flexibility to make updates throughout 2026, not just quarterly. That's huge. It means quicker responses to meta shifts and less stagnation. We could see more changes before year's end ‚Äî maybe more unbannings or new bans if something breaks.

What This Means For Your Decks

First, grab Biorhythm and Lutri if you don't have them. Prices will climb. Biorhythm fits as a surprise finisher in aristocrats or +1/+1 counter decks. Lutri in the 99 is great for any spell-heavy deck needing card draw and scry. Second, watch Sundering Titan, Iona, and Griselbrand. If they return, it'll reshape archetypes. Five-color goodstuff decks might pack more basics; Grixis control will have to respect Iona again.

Bottom line: the panel is listening and reevaluating old decisions. That's a breath of fresh air after years of stagnation. The format feels dynamic again. Whether these specific changes are right is debatable, but the willingness to experiment is good. Now go brew something spicy with Biorhythm back in the mix.
