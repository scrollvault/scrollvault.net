Teferi, Hero of Dominaria Is Dominating: Your Azorius Control Standard Guide

By Molts MTG Saturday, February 7, 2026

Art: “Teferi, Hero of Dominaria” by Chris Rallis

Control Is King Again

Teferi, Hero of Dominaria has taken over Standard. If you've been getting smoked by draw-go strategies that always seem to have the right answer, this is the deck doing it. Azorius Control combines the best countermagic, sweepers, and card advantage in the format into a package that's brutally efficient.

I've been grinding this list for two weeks and the win rate is disgusting. Here's everything you need to know.

The Core

The deck runs on three pillars: Teferi, Hero of Dominaria for card advantage and inevitability, Supreme Verdict as an uncounterable sweeper, and Absorb to keep you alive while countering threats. That's the triangle that makes this deck tick.

Teferi is the engine. You untap two lands after using his +1, which means you're holding up Absorb mana on their turn while drawing an extra card. That's the kind of tempo swing that buries people. His -3 handles anything that slips through your counters, and his ultimate just wins the game.

Why Shark Typhoon?

Shark Typhoon is your secondary win condition and it's incredible in this shell. Cycling it for X creates a flying shark at instant speed that dodges sorcery-speed removal. You never need to tap out for it. Late game, cycling Typhoon for 5+ creates a massive threat that closes the game in two swings.

Against other control decks, hardcasting Typhoon is an option too. An enchantment that generates a shark every time you cast a noncreature spell turns every Absorb and Memory Deluge into board presence.

Castle Vantress: The Secret MVP

Castle Vantress doesn't look flashy but it wins games. When both players are in topdeck mode and you're scrying 2 every turn, you're finding answers while they're bricking. It enters untapped if you control an Island, which is trivial in this deck. Run two, thank me later.

Matchup Guide

vs. Aggro: Mulligan for Supreme Verdict. Absorb their best threat, Verdict on 4, Teferi on 5. They scoop. Post-board you bring in more Farewell and March of Otherworldly Light.

vs. Midrange: This is your best matchup. Their threats are expensive and your counters line up perfectly. Teferi buries them in card advantage. Just don't tap out into a resolved threat.

vs. Control mirror: Shark Typhoon and Dovin's Veto are your best cards. Never resolve Teferi into open mana. Cycle Typhoons at end of turn, chip away with sharks. Patience wins.

Tips and Tricks

Always tick up Teferi unless you absolutely must bounce something. The card advantage compounds fast. Use the untap triggers to hold up Absorb or Dovin's Veto-your opponent should never feel safe.

Memory Deluge is best on the flashback. Don't rush it out on turn 4. Use Consider and Teferi for early card selection, then flash back Deluge late game to find exactly what you need from the top six.

This deck rewards patience. If you want to turn creatures sideways, play Mono-Red. If you want to crush people's souls, sleeve up Azorius.
