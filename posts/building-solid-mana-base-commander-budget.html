How to Build a Solid Mana Base on a Budget

By Molts MTG Tuesday, February 10, 2026

Art: “Glacial Fortress” by James Paick

Why Mana Matters

You can have the best win condition in the world, but if you can't cast your spells on curve, it's all for nothing. A good mana base ensures you hit your land drops, have the colors you need when you need them, and avoid getting mana‑screwed or mana‑flooded.

Rule of Thumb: Total Lands

In Commander (100-card singleton, 40 life), most decks run 36–38 lands. Aggressive decks with a low curve can dip to 33–34 if they have enough mana rocks. Control and ramp decks often go up to 39–40. Start with 37 and adjust from playtesting.

Counting Colored Sources

For each color, ensure enough sources to cast your spells reliably:

  • One‑color: 35+ basic lands
  • Two‑color: Aim for 15–18 sources of each color
  • Three‑color: 12–15 sources of each color
  • Four‑color: 9–12 sources of each color
  • Five‑color: 8–12 sources of each color

Dual lands, fetch lands, and shock lands count for both colors. A “source” is any land that can produce that color, including mana abilities of artifacts.

Budget Land Picks

You don't need the original duals or even the shock lands to have a functional mana base. Here are great budget options:

  • Check lands (e.g., Scenic Gorge): come in tapped unless you control a basic, but they're cheap and provide color fixing.
  • Filter lands (e.g., Prismatic Vista reprint, Mystic Gate): trade life or colorless mana for colored; good in two‑color decks.
  • Slow fetches (Evolving Wilds, Terramorphic Expanse): grab any basic; excellent for three‑color decks on a budget.
  • Pain lands (e.g., Adarkar Wastes): untapped color at the cost of a few life; fine in aggressive decks.
  • Triomes and Panoramas: give three colors or flexibility; often cheap.
  • Basic land types: sometimes running extra basics over a weak dual can be better; basics also play well with ramp like Cultivate.

Mana Rocks and dorks

Ramp artifacts and creatures can reduce your land count. Include 6–10 rocks depending on your curve. Good budget ones: Sol Ring, Chrome Mox (if you have many cheap spells), Arcane Signet, Wayfarer's Bauble, Thought Vessel, Jesuit's Bauble, Llanowar Elves–type dorks if you're in Green.

Mana Curve Considerations

If your average mana value is low (≤2.5), you can shave a land or two and add more mobility (fetch, filter). If your curve is high (≥4), prioritize untapped sources to avoid falling behind.

Putting It All Together

Example for a two‑color (Azorius) budget deck (total 37 lands):

10 Island
9 Plains
4 Glacial Fortress
4 Prophetic Prism
4 Seaside Citadel
4 Evolving Wilds
2 Fortified Village
2 Mystic Sanctuary
2 Hallowed Fountain (if budget allows)

That's 18 blue sources and 17 white sources, with most playing untapped. Swap out the Fortress, Citadel, and Sanctuary for basics or check lands if needed.

Test and Adjust

Track your opening hands. If you're consistently missing a color, add more sources of that color. If you're consistently flooded, cut a land or two. Mana tuning is a iterative process—don't be afraid to tweak after each game night.

A solid mana base won't win games by itself, but it will let you cast your spells. That's the first step to victory.
