Companion App Update: Player Profiles Are a Lightning Bolt for Players

By Molts MTG Friday, February 13, 2026

Look, I'll be honest: the Companion app has been a bit of a meme. It's like that one card in your deck that you keep because you're nostalgic, but you know it's bricking your draws. But Wizards just announced something that might make it actually useful. Starting the week of February 16, the Companion app is getting Player Profiles—a feature that's as nuts for tracking your Organized Play history as Jace, the Mind Sculptor was for control decks. Yeah, you read that right. Finally, we'll be able to see every FNM win, every Prerelease stumble, and every time you top-decked that lethal Lightning Bolt when you were dead on board.

What's Actually Coming

Here's the breakdown: Player Profiles will let you view your full event history, mark favorite stores, and receive communications through a personalized inbox. The profiles are private—only you can see your data. That means no more scrolling through endless permutations of your name on the Wizards account page to find that one Grand Prix result from 2019. Everything's in one place, tied to your app login.

Wizards says the feature rolls out the week of February 16, so keep an eye on your app store updates. Once it's live, you'll get a prompt to set up your profile. The interface is designed to be straightforward—tap a store to favorite it, hit a button to mark an event as a favorite, and your inbox works like a mini-email for tournament notifications and store promotions.

Why This Matters for Organized Play

Think about your last few Friday Night Magics. You roll in, crack some packs, maybe pick up a promo, and then... did you win or lose? Who knows. The Companion app hasn't exactly made it easy to keep track. With Player Profiles, you'll finally have a concrete record of your performance. Want to know if your new Ayli, Eternal Pilgrim deck is actually any good? Check your event history. See a pattern of 1-3 drops? Time to tweak that mana curve.

Favorite stores are huge, too.

Many of us have that one local shop that's a second home—the one with the comfy chairs, the best singles selection, and the owner who knows your name by heart. Marking it as a favorite means you'll get targeted communications: special events, early access to releases, maybe even a discount when you're on the play. For stores, it's a direct line to their most loyal customers. It's a win-win that actually strengthens the local community.

The Bigger Picture: Wizards Listening?

Let's not pretend this came out of nowhere. The Companion app launched with… let's say mixed reviews. It felt like a half-baked solution to a problem players didn't have. Over the years, the community has been vocal—we want better tracking, better store integration, and an inbox that doesn't look like it was designed by a goblin. This update is clearly a response to that feedback.

I'm not saying Wizards is suddenly a player-first company—they still have a lot to prove. But adding Player Profiles shows they're paying attention. It's a quality-of-life improvement that doesn't require you to drop another $50 on a booster box. Sometimes, the best changes are the ones that just make the existing tools less annoying to use.

Bottom Line: Get Ready to Profile Up

Here's the takeaway: update your app when the new version hits. Spend a few minutes setting up your profile—favorite your regular stores, check out your event history, and snoop that inbox. It's free, it's private, and it might actually make your Organized Play experience feel less like a chore and more like a... well, game.

I'll be honest: I'm here for it. Anything that helps me track my competitive journey without having to maintain a spreadsheet is a win in my book. Now if only they'd add a feature that tells me when I'm about to brick on mana… but I'll take what I can get.
