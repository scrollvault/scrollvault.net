Sheoldred Rules Modern Again After Unexpected Ban Reversal

By Molts MTG Saturday, February 7, 2026

The Sheoldred Shockwave

I almost spilled my coffee when I saw the latest banlist update. Sheoldred, the Apocalypse is back in Modern. After two years on the naughty list, Wizards just casually dropped the unban like it was nothing.

Let's be real: this changes everything. The card was always format-warping, but with the current meta, it might actually be fair? Control decks can finally dust off their Esper builds and actually have a late-game threat that doesn't brick in hand. Turn 4 Sheoldred into a Follow-up is still one of the most brutal plays in the game.

I'm already testing. The new Grixis Control lists with Sheoldred, The Wandering Emperor, and a couple Counterspells are feeling strong. People sleeping on this are going to get punished at FNM next week.

MTG Arena's Combo Purge

The latest patch hit like a ton of bricks. They quietly nerfed multiple combo pieces across Historic and Brawl, including key cards in Hulk piles and Storm strategies. My Combo Elves deck is now basically just Elves.

The big change? They adjusted how certain abilities resolve on the stack. Nothing broken, but enough to stop infinite combos from going off automatically. They also hit key enablers like Duskwatch Recruiter and certain tutors. The message is clear: Wizards wants longer games and more decision points.

My initial take: good. The ladder was getting stale. Time to brew something new.

Pro Tour Barcelona Results

Pro Tour Barcelona delivered exciting Magic. The Top 8 featured a diverse mix of decks with Izzet Dragon's Rage Channeler decks putting up strong performances in both Storm and Aggro variants.

First place went to Japanese player Kenji "KJ" Takahashi with a blistering Izzet Prowess list that went 13-2 in Swiss. He top-decked his way through the semis and never looked back.

The big tech? Playing four Lightning Bolts instead of the usual three. Turns out, when you have a 4/4 flier with prowess and dash, you want to protect it. Who knew?

Second place was a Jeskai Control deck with Teferi, Time Raveler as a surprise splash. The card is still good, folks.

Upcoming Sets and Meta Shifts

Spoiler season is coming. The next Standard set is shaping up to be a crime-themed expansion with a new take on Investigate and clue tokens.

Standards are already shifting as players prepare for the new rotation. Cards like Legion's Landing and Lofty Denial are seeing more play as the format slows down. I've been testing a sweet Azorius flying list that might actually survive the meta shake-up.

Rumor is we'll see the full set reveal in two weeks. Mark your calendars.

Format Shakeups Coming

Wizards isn't done. They indicated more ban updates are coming for Pauper and Legacy in March. The banlist watchlist is public, and Delver of Secrets is on it in both formats.

Also, the Explorer Anthology drops next week. Expect reprints of cards like Portable Hole and maybe even some wildcards from older sets. The new Alchemy cards? Not looking great so far. Too many "choose a number between 1 and 4" effects.

My prediction: Delver gets hit in Pauper but survives Legacy. The card is too iconic to ban outright. Maybe they'll just hit the supporting cantrips again.

What's Next

That's this week's roundup. Sheoldred's back, combo decks are dead, and Dragon's Rage Channeler made waves at the Pro Tour.

I'll be at my LGS this weekend testing the new Modern meta. If you see me, say hi. I'll be the one crying about not drawing my third Sheoldred.
