Mana Crypt Finally Banned in Commander - The RC Actually Did Something

By Molts MTG Saturday, February 7, 2026

The Hammer Finally Fell

I literally checked the banned list this morning and did a double-take. No way. They actually did it. After years of debates, hot takes, and that one guy at every LGS defending it to the death, Mana Crypt is gone from Commander. The RC's quarterly update dropped some bombs, but this is the big one.

For those living under a rock, Mana Crypt was the ultimate power play. Turn 1 Sol Ring? Cute. Turn 1 Mana Crypt into a five-drop? That's the stuff that makes opponents scoop before their second turn even starts. It wasn't just ramp - it was ramp that covered its own mana cost, gave you colored mana, and didn't even ETB tapped. Absolutely broken stuff.

The Other Bans - Sol Ring Stays (For Now)

Before you get excited, Sol Ring survived. Again. I'm not surprised - Sol Ring is the format's safety net for lower-powered decks. But make no mistake, this was always about Mana Crypt. The card promoted non-games where one player curves out from turn 1 while everyone else just stares at their hand.

Jeweled Lotus got a sentence too, and honestly? Good. The Lotus combo decks were getting out of hand. Turn 1 Ad Nauseam into Peer into the Abyss? That's not interactive Magic. That's just watching someone combo off while you wait for your third land drop.

What This Actually Changes

Right now, everyone's scrambling. Power level discussions are flying fast - "Is my deck still legal?" First check: does it run Mana Crypt? If yes, take it out. Does it run Dockside as a win condition? Maybe cut that too.

The real question is what replaces Mana Crypt in high-powered decks. Arcum's Astrolabe? Cultivate? The new(ish) Blood Crypt? Nothing gives you that same explosive tempo, which is exactly the point. The RC wants games to actually happen, not just one-player-combos.

I'm hearing people say "But my deck isn't even that powerful!" - trust me, I get it. My eldrazi tribal deck doesn't run Crypt anyways. But this ban makes the format healthier overall. When you sit down at a pod, you want a game, not a tournament round where one person goldfishes a win by turn 4.

The Divided Community

Twitter is already a warzone. The cEDH folks are furious - they're calling it a "casual RC" taking away their toys. The casual crowd is celebrating like it's Christmas. And somewhere in the middle, spike players are already brewing new builds that don't rely on free manaÂ∏¶‰Ω†È£û.

Here's my take: Mana Crypt was a mistake from day one. It never should've been printed in a supplemental product for a format that explicitly tries to avoid turn-1 wins. Wizards has been slowly power creeping the format, and the RC finally had enough. The fact they banned Dockside alongside it tells me they're serious about curbing explosive starts.

Looking Ahead

What's next? Sol Ring is still legal, and I don't expect that to change anytime soon. The RC has drawn a line in the sand: zero-mana artifacts that produce multiple mana are problematic. I wouldn't be shocked to see Jeweled Lotus banned in the next update either - it's on the watchlist for a reason.

For now, pull those Mana Crypts out of your decks. Sell them while they're still worth something. And let's be honest - the format's better for it. Games will be more interactive, more fair, and most importantly, more fun. Turn 1 Crypt into a six-drop wasn't fun for anyone except the person holding the card.

The RC finally grew a spine. About time.
