Bloodletter of Aclazotz Shines in Roll for Initiative Secret Lair

By Molts MTG Saturday, February 7, 2026

Secret Lair Meets D&D Again

Wizards of the Coast is tapping into the Forgotten Realms once more with the "Roll for Initiative" Secret Lair Superdrop, arriving February 9. This collection of seven themed drops reprints cards from previous D&D sets, mainly Commander Legends: Battle for Baldur's Gate. While some drops are straightforward, one card is already causing a stir: Bloodletter of Aclazotz, the $30 vampire demon that could become a Commander staple.

What's in the Seven Drops?

The superdrop splits into themed bundles ranging from $29.99 to $39.99. Each includes five cards plus basics (or in one case, a Dragon). Here's the lineup:

  • Shadowheart's Devotion: black-focused sacrifice synergy with Shadowheart, Dark Justiciar, and the expensive Ancient Bronze Dragon (~$16).
  • Gale's Ambition: blue spells like Brainstorm, Arcane Denial, and Personal Tutor. Not huge value unless you love Gale.
  • Black Lights & Dark Dungeons: features Displacer Kitten (~$24) and Sefris of the Hidden Ways, plus Tortured Existence. Great for dungeon venture fans.
  • Shadows Over Baldur's Gate: Prosper, Tome-Bound package with Xorn, Wild-Magic Sorcerer, and Grim Hireling ($16-$18).
  • Whispers in Candlekeep: Counterspell, dragon cards like Klauth and Miirym. Solid but niche.
  • Strahd's Descent: vampire demon Strefan, Bloodletter of Aclazotz, End the Festivities, and Castle Ravenloft. This is the money drop.
  • Lands of the Forgotten Realms: five themed basic land arts. Pure cosmetics.

If you're after pure card value, the Strahd's Descent and Shadowheart's Devotion drops stand out.

Bloodletter of Aclazotz: The $30 Highlight

Originally from The Lost Caverns of Ixalan, Bloodletter of Aclazotz has become a Commander favorite for life-loss decks. At {1}{B}{B}{B} for a 2/4 flying creature, its ability reads: "If an opponent would lose life during your turn, they lose twice that much life instead." That's a massive multiplier. Pair it with cards like Sanguine Bond, Vampiric Nectar, or any effect that drains life, and you're looking at one-shot kills.

In Edgar Markov or any black vampire tribal, this card accelerates your game plan dramatically. It's not bulk rare by any stretch-single copies hover around $30, making the $29.99 Secret Lair price a no-brainer if you want the card and the other four cards in the drop (Strefan, End the Festivities, Castle Ravenloft, and the alternate art).

Some might argue that a 4-drop with a conditional ability is slow, but in a format like Commander where games go long, doubling life loss can close out matches quickly. I've seen it turn a 5-life drain into 10 and win out of nowhere.

Other Cards Worth Your Attention

Beyond Bloodletter, a few other cards justify the price:

  • Ancient Bronze Dragon: A 7/7 flying dragon for {7} that rolls a d20 and distributes +1/+1 counters. It's a fun, powerful finisher that currently sells for $16+.
  • Displacer Kitten: Blink decks love this cat. Every noncreature spell blinked ETB triggers. At ~$24, it's a key piece for many Commander strategies.
  • Grim Hireling: Treasure generation and a sink to shrink creatures. A solid add-on in Korvold or any treasure deck.

If you already own these singles, the drops might still be worth it for the alternate art, but check current prices before buying.

Is It Worth Buying?

Secret Lair prices these drops at $30 (non-foil) and $40 (foil). That's steep if you only want one card. However, many of these cards have appreciated over time. The convenience of getting a themed bundle plus basic lands (in the case of the Lands drop) adds some value.

My take: If you play Commander and lack Bloodletter of Aclazotz, grab the Strahd's Descent drop. Even at $30, you're getting $30+ worth of card plus three extras. For players who already have the high-value singles, the drops are harder to justify unless you're a collector or love the D&D alt art.

Also consider that these are print-to-demand; they won't skyrocket in price like limited drops. The market might stabilize, so no rush to flip.

Wrap-Up

The "Roll for Initiative" Superdrop is a solid offering for Commander players, especially those who missed the first D&D crossover. Bloodletter of Aclazotz is the clear star, but cards like Ancient Bronze Dragon and Displacer Kitten add serious value. Mark your calendar for February 9, and decide quick-these drops aren't limited, but the $30 price point might creep up if demand surges.
